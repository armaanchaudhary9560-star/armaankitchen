Ball Joint

Ball joints are pivot-type connectors that link control arms to the steering knuckles, allowing smooth movement and flexibility in the suspension system. They enable the wheels to turn left and right while also moving up and down with the suspension. Enclosed in a lubricated housing, ball joints withstand high loads and constant motion. Over time, they may wear out due to friction and environmental exposure, leading to steering looseness, uneven tire wear, or suspension noise. Regular inspection and timely replacement are important for safety. Quality ball joints ensure precise steering control, smooth suspension operation, and improved handling on all road conditions.
